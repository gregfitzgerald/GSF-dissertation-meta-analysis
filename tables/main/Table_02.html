Table 2

Resource Level Scale (RLS) Categories

CategoryDescriptionEthological Basis
1. Social PartnersPresence of conspecifics beyond single housingMice and rats are social species; wild rodents live in groups
2. Running WheelsVoluntary access to exercise wheelsRodents run 5-10 km/night when given wheel access
3. Additional Floor SpaceCage floor area exceeding standard dimensionsPermits territory establishment and reduces crowding stress
4. Vertical SpacePlatforms, climbing structures, or multi-level environmentsStandard cage height prevents rats from standing upright
5. Nesting MaterialPaper, cotton, or other substrates for nest constructionNest building is a highly motivated behavior
6. Shelters/Hiding PlacesEnclosed spaces, igloos, tunnels, or opaque containersWild rodents spend substantial time in burrows
7. Chewable ToysWood blocks, cardboard, or other gnawing substratesContinuous incisor growth requires gnawing
8. Manipulable ObjectsBalls, plastic items, or other movable objectsObject manipulation provides cognitive challenge
9. Ramps/LaddersInclined surfaces connecting different cage levelsIncreases locomotor opportunities and spatial complexity
10. Foraging OpportunitiesScattered food, puzzle feeders, or substrates requiring searchWild rodents spend substantial time foraging
11. Novelty/VarietyRegular rotation or replacement of environmental featuresNovel objects trigger exploratory behavior

Note. Categories adapted from Cait et al. (2022, 2024). Each category scored dichotomously (0 = absent, 1 = present) for control and intervention conditions.
